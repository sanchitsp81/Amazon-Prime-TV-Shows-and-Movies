# Amazon Prime Video EDA

## Project Overview

This project conducts Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Amazon Prime Video dataset to understand the platform's content library. It explores the distribution of Movies and TV Shows, genres, ratings, release trends, production countries, and other key features. The goal is to identify meaningful patterns and generate insights that can support data-driven content and business decisions.

## Dataset Information
The dataset contains information on Movies and TV Shows available on Amazon Prime Video. It consists of two CSV files:
titles.csv – Contains details about the titles, such as genre, release year, runtime, ratings, and other metadata.
credits.csv – Contains information about the cast and crew, including actors and directors associated with each title.

## Project Objectives
Analyze the distribution of Movies and TV Shows on Amazon Prime Video.
Study content growth and trends over the years.
Identify the most common genres and categories.
Explore content ratings and certifications.
Examine content production by country.
Analyze movie durations and TV show seasons.
Identify the highest-rated titles using IMDb and TMDb ratings.
Discover the most frequently featured actors and directors.
Explore the relationship between ratings, popularity, and runtime.
Assess the overall diversity of the Amazon Prime Video content library.

## Key Insights
Amazon Prime Video's library is dominated by Movies, with fewer TV Shows.
Most titles have IMDb ratings between 6 and 8, indicating good overall quality.
Highly rated (8+) content makes up a smaller share of the library.
Content is primarily produced in the United States, India, and the United Kingdom.
TV Shows generally receive slightly higher and more consistent ratings than Movies.
A few directors contribute multiple titles, indicating creator concentration.
Ratings and votes are strongly associated with popularity, while runtime has little influence.

## Future Scope
Expand investment in TV Shows to improve user engagement.
Focus on acquiring and producing more high-rated content.
Increase content diversity by investing in underrepresented regions.
Enhance recommendation systems using ratings and popularity metrics.
Promote high-quality but less popular titles to improve discoverability.
Apply predictive analytics and sentiment analysis to support content planning and audience insights.


## Conclusion
This EDA provides valuable insights into Amazon Prime Video's content library, highlighting trends in content distribution, genres, ratings, and production. The findings suggest opportunities to improve content quality, diversify regional offerings, and expand TV show content to enhance user engagement and support future business growth.
